HIRE ME

Dream big

Next-Level Training developer

“Climbing, in general, does a pretty good job strengthening your lats, pecs and forearm muscles,” says Chi Harris, a Portland, Oregon-based coach. “But what gets overlooked are the antagonistic muscles—the ones that get used opposite your main climbing muscles.” If you only use one set of muscles in a really specific way, “you get pulled out of normalcy, so your body is out of equilibrium.” Powerfingers helps fix this. To counter all the grabbing you’re doing while climbing, these bands wrap around your fingers and spread them apart, so you’re forced to work the rest of the muscles in your forearms.
